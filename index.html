More in-depth

My name is T-Jay and currently I live in the great city of Nashville with my girlfriend and our two dogs (Zoey and Oscar) where I ply my trade at Emma, the awesome email marketing software platform. I have been interested in all things web since I got my first internet connection circa 1997. I've been tinkering, exploring and building web-related things for nearly two decades. Being artistic by nature, I have a keen sense of design which sets me a bit apart from most developers.

In my spare time, I enjoy Crossfit, rock climbing, ice hockey and other sports. There's also the music thing; I have been a professional vocalist, songwriter, guitarist, and producer for almost fifteen years now. I front the band Haven Fire currently, but was also the frontman for Wake the Light, with whom I signed my first recording contract. I've been fortunate to tour through quite a few awesome places, but Puerto Peñasco, Sonora, Mexico might be my favorite.

I build robust, complex web applications using proven methods and technologies that are best suited to the task.

The work I do

Over the course of my career thus far, I've been fortunate to work on some exciting projects for some pretty awesome clients both as a freelancer and as a full-time agency employee.

Just as I have a diverse range of skills, my contributions for these clients and their projects were varied. Everything from pure design work to front-end development to complex architecture and functional development.

I'm also quite active in developing open source applications and technologies, out of my own personal curiosity and necessities but also for community consumption. For example, I developed a first-of-its-kind application to parse API Blueprint files and create a living back-end service complete with a database for CRUD operations. Check out some of my other dabblings by looking at my repositories.

I'm available for freelance or contract work, for everything from simple design work all the way up to constructing your application in its entirety and helping you get it deployed out into the wild.
